Helpful Information for Construction Volunteers

  • We work on Tuesday and Thursday from 8:00 a.m. to noon and on Saturday from 8:00 a.m. to 12 p.m.

Safety:

  • All construction volunteers must be over 18 years of age.
  • Construction site workers, volunteers and visitors must wear closed-toe shoes.
  • The Construction Manager will cover safety guidelines at the beginning of each work shift. It is crucial to be on time to receive this important training.
  • Water, cool drinks and snacks are available at all times. A work break is at 10:00 a.m. Saturday workers staying until 3 p.m. should bring a lunch.
  • Volunteers assist with tasks such as basic carpentry, painting, sanding, hanging drywall, installing siding or laying sod. Tasks vary as the house is completed.

What to bring and wear:

  • Wear work clothes or clothing you do not mind getting dirty.
  • Boots or other comfortable closed-toed shoes, like running shoes, are required.
  • The sun can be intense. You may want to bring a hat and sunscreen. Water, cold drinks and snacks are provided on site.
  • All tools and materials are provided. Volunteers do not need hammers or other tools, but you are welcome to bring your tool belt if you wish.

End of shift:

  • If everyone pitches in, cleaning up the site at the end of the shift goes quickly for all. Please help pick up trash and reorganize materials.
  • Return all equipment to the tool trailer or secure as directed by the Construction Manager.
