“I would do all it over again in a heartbeat!”

Kristen is a single mom with three kids who has lived in Citrus County her whole life. After going through a divorce, Kristen’s world turned upside down. She still kept her job, but she lost her house…the house where her kids felt safe, the house that provided stability and helped keep her family together.

In January 2010, Kristen heard about the Habitat for Humanity’s orientation and went to listen. She was intrigued, but did not fill out the application. She’d heard of the good work Habitat had done with affordable housing, but she wasn’t sure it was for her. After research and soul searching, Kristen went to another orientation and applied. She is so glad she did.

Her first day at the construction site of her new home was intimidating. Kristen didn’t even know how to swing a hammer. She says, “Everyone was so helpful. They showed you how to do things. They were terrific teachers.”

Kristin said, “It really is an inspiring program. Habitat changed my life. I would do it all over again in a heartbeat. It was so hard on my kids to be living with family members. But when we got a Habitat house, it really benefitted them. Their schoolwork and their attitudes improved.”

“The people at Habitat are some of the most unselfish people I’ve ever met. They are committed to having people do well for themselves.”

Kristen’s house was dedicated in June 2011.
