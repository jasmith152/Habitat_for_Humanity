It seems we are outwitting the summers heat.   Two steps forward and one back.   I'm talking about the humid and rainy weather that has deluged us in recent weeks.   We have Kenny taping and plastering and he is doing a diligent job.  We just can't get the coats to dry in this humid weather.   As of now we will resume working on the 22nd of July, a tuesday.   About a week late from our schedule.  During this interim the septic system was installed and we contacted the contractor to install the drive and walks.

We met with the Partners for the Roger's home, selected the trees that are to remain and have scheduled Debusk to clear the site.   It is possible that because of the elevation, in comparison the street, we may have to go to a stem wall foundation.   This is pouring a footing, setting building blocks, filling the cavity with sand, on which the concrete is poured.  If this is necessary, we will get a cost for the process and report the amount to the board in our next monthly report.

Norm Peterson, Mike Crusco and I met with a draftsman who has agreed to help us consolidate the drafting and engineering processes.   Both are residents of Citrus County.   Before we were using an engineer who was located in Clearwater and a draftsman from our county.  Our thanks to both parties who helped us in the past.  

For the Committee,
Terry McMillan
