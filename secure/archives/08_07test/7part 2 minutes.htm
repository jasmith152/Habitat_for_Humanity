Habitat for Humanity Citrus County, Inc

DRAFT BOARD MEETING MINUTES

Meeting Place  :   Citrus County Chronicle Building tt1624 N. Meadowcrest Blvd.t Crystal River, Fl 34429
Date                   :   June 26, 2003
Time                  :   6:40 P.M. – 7:45 P.M.

 The following Board members were present:  Norm Peterson, John Thrumston,  Michelle Maidlow, Bonnie Peterson, Gil Brier, Mike Crusco, Linda Deptola, Dolly Dougan, Dan Groner, Terry McMillan, Otis Long, Roseann Strawn, Mark Whittaker

Also in attendance were: Executive Director Lorna Nimmo,  Judy Groner

A quorum was established.

 

The meeting was reconvened by President elect Norm Peterson.

 

Legal/Finance Committee

Norm observed and stated that the Legal/Finance Committee is not functioning in our current affiliate structure as a standing committee, and it might be practical to reassign it’s functionality to an advisory committee. Discussion concluded that the expertise of this committee should be called upon on an “as need” basis.

MOTION:

John Thrumston moved to rename the committee to Legal/Finance Advisory Committee

SECONDED BY:

Dolly Dougan

VOTE:

Unanimous in favor

 

Appointment and Confirmation of Operating Committee Chairs

In regards to operating committee staffing, the fiscal year began with the appointment and confirmation of Committee Chairs. Norm asked for board affirmation of the appointments. Discussion ensued about the structure of the committees, and the need for Vice Chairs to be appointed by the committee chairs.  

 

Apparent was the number of committees with no staffing at this point in time. It was mentioned that recruitment for committees should be a priority and is the responsibility of the committee chairs and all board members.

 

Mark W. raised the issue of  HFHCC transitioning towards a “governing board” as opposed to the current “working board” structure. Lorna and Norm affirmed the intent to move in that direction, but that currently, the affiliate infrastructure does not allow for this to be possible. It is a goal that needs to be in place as part of the strategic plan. 

MOTION:

Linda Deptola moved to accept operating committee chairs Dan Groner for Site Selection; Terry McMillan for Building; Linda Deptola for Family Services and Gene Reuman for Church Relations as presented.

SECONDED BY:

Gil Brier

VOTE:

Unanimous in favor

 
 

Establishment of Special Ad Hoc Committees

The two ad hoc committees in place are the Nominating Committee and the Strategic Planning Committee. Norm proposed that they remain in place and, that moving forward, the Vice President take on the responsibility of bringing together any and all ad hoc committees for initial meetings for the purpose of electing Chairs/Vice Chairs. Ongoing, the V.P. will participate in ad hoc meetings and act as liaison to the Executive Committee reporting issues and progress of ad hoc committees. Both committees are seeking volunteers.

MOTION:

John Thrumston moved to carry special ad hoc Committees forward into the upcoming fiscal year.

SECONDED BY:

Mike Crusco

VOTE:

Unanimous in favor

 
 

Review Yearly Budget

The 2003-2004 Budget was reviewed in an effort to familiarize new board members with it. Since the budget was derived in the previous fiscal year, under departed leadership, it was announced that any newly conceived business (i.e. appreciation events; special PR campaigns etc) that may require affiliate funds will necessitate special fund raising efforts, and the advent of such activities will be written up in a detailed plan and presented to the board for approval.

 

Old Business:

Mark W. would like to start early this year on his “Alternative Christmas” donation/fund raiser. His church alone raised ~$7000. in donations that came to HFHCC as well as Society of St. Andrews and The Heffer Project. He is looking to spread the word about the plan and its guidelines to all of the Citrus County religious community during the months of July & August this year in an effort to foster more participation and result in more donations. He needs a current and updated list of churches. (See Action Item 0302)

 

Dan brought up an issue of Title and transfer fees that the affiliate could pay for property purchases. The dollar amount being minimal, it was decided that procedurally,  those fees should come out of the Site Selection Committee operating budget, and any future concerns about particular or unique purchases be brought before the Executive Committee.

 

A question was raised about the Toner Fund Raiser program. Lorna has questioned the integrity of this program and will look into it for ongoing participation of the affiliate. (See Action Item 0304)

 

New Business:

Set number of Board Members for the new year.

MOTION:

Terry McMillan moved set the number of board members at sixteen (16) for the 2003-2004 fiscal year.

SECONDED BY:

John Thrumston

VOTE:

Unanimous in favor

 

Norm Peterson appointed Dan Groner as an “At Large’ Executive Board member.

 

As a positive affirmation the board reviewed the affiliate Mission Statement. Norm then stated that his expectation is for every board member to participate in a minimum of four (4) hours on every Habitat home construction site. Thus personally committing to action the words and deeds of our mission.

 

There being no further business to bring forth to this Board, a Motion to adjourn was made my Gil Brier. It was seconded by Mike Crusco and approved by all.

 

Respectfully submitted,

Bonnie Peterson

Bonnie Peterson, Secretary
